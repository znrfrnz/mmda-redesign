# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Metro Manila residents, commuters, and citizens who need to access government services, check real-time traffic conditions, read advisories, and report road concerns. They range from daily commuters checking EDSA traffic at 6am on their phones to office workers filing vehicle-related paperwork on desktop. Many are bilingual (English/Filipino) with varying digital literacy. Context: often rushed, often on mobile, often frustrated by government bureaucracy. The job to be done is getting accurate, timely public service information without fighting the interface.

## Product Purpose

A modern, accessible redesign of mmda.gov.ph that fixes eight documented HCI problems (cluttered navigation, poor hierarchy, inaccessible forms, external redirects, inconsistent branding). Built as a working prototype for an HCI course demonstrating how citizen-first design principles transform a government portal. Success looks like: a user can find traffic status, read an advisory, or start a service request within seconds, not minutes.

## Brand Personality

Professional, Accessible, Filipino.

Voice is authoritative but never cold. Tone is direct and helpful, like a competent civil servant who respects your time. No bureaucratic jargon, no marketing fluff. Filipino civic identity expressed through restrained use of national colors (blue, red, gold) and bilingual content, not through decorative cultural motifs.

## Anti-references

- **The original mmda.gov.ph**: Cluttered navigation, hover-triggered dropdowns, walls of competing elements, inconsistent typography, external Google Form redirects, missing alt text, no keyboard nav, outdated "Twitter" branding.
- **Typical bloated GovPH portals**: Information overload, auto-playing carousels, banner fatigue, tiny text on busy backgrounds, tables that break on mobile, redundant sidebars.
- **Generic AI-generated government templates**: Cookie-cutter hero sections, stock photo grids, gradient text, glassmorphism, identical card grids with icon + heading + description repeated endlessly, dark-blue-and-white "government look" pulled from training data.

## Design Principles

1. **Respect the citizen's time.** Every interaction should be faster than the equivalent on the original site. No unnecessary steps, no cognitive overhead.
2. **One thing at a time.** Each section has one clear purpose and one primary action. Competing elements are eliminated, not rearranged.
3. **Trustworthy by default.** All services are native (no external form redirects). Visual consistency and proper accessibility signal institutional competence.
4. **Filipino first, not Filipino-themed.** Bilingual content is a first-class feature, not a toggle buried in settings. National identity is expressed through substance (language, civic utility), not decoration.
5. **Accessible is non-negotiable.** WCAG AA contrast, full keyboard navigation, screen reader support, and progressive font scaling are baseline, not extras.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA compliance for all text/background combinations.
- Full keyboard navigation: dropdowns, dialogs, mobile menu, all interactive elements.
- Visible focus indicators on every focusable element.
- Screen reader support: semantic HTML, ARIA labels on icon-only buttons, meaningful alt text.
- Bilingual EN/FIL with full translation coverage on every page.
- Font size scaling (3 levels) and high-contrast mode toggle.
- Skip-to-content link.
- Reduced motion: minimal animations only (fade-in, hover states), no scroll-triggered effects.
